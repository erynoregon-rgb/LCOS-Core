# Artifact-Backed Governance for Tool-Using AI Agents

## One-line position

I make AI agent work auditable from artifacts instead of summaries.

## Problem

AI coding agents and tool-using assistants increasingly perform real work, but their outputs often leave false state behind:

- "done" summaries without committed evidence
- tools that are built but not wired into live paths
- tests that pass locally but do not prove the claimed behavior
- dirty repo/runtime state that is not classified
- missing recovery paths after high-risk changes
- summaries treated as evidence

The safety problem is not only whether a model gives a good answer. It is whether the surrounding workflow can make the agent's work bounded, auditable, replayable, and correctable.

## Public artifact

LCOS-Core is my public reduced scaffold. It demonstrates mechanism classes such as:

- append-oriented receipt ledgers
- typed hold / escalate / reject / accept decisions
- deterministic toy routing with visible reasons
- replay and timeline rendering
- schema-light validation
- adversarial synthetic fixtures
- explicit public/private disclosure boundaries

LCOS-Core is not the full private system. It is a claim-safe public proof-of-mechanism.

## Private substrate

SKOS-Core is the broader private/local research substrate where I develop the larger governance workflow:

- work cells
- preflight and postflight
- semantic recovery
- local operational evidence
- publication boundaries
- receipts and closeouts
- system-state truth maintenance

The private substrate is not transferred, disclosed, or controlled through this sponsorship.

## Why now

Teams are adopting coding agents faster than they are building state-truth systems around them. The immediate need is not another chatbot wrapper; it is a way to know what an agent actually changed, whether the change was wired, what proof exists, what remains dirty, and what claims are unsupported.

This work turns that problem into artifacts: receipts, leases, closeouts, replay, postflight gates, and claim-safe public scaffolds.
